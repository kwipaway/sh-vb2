Federal Benefits (VA)

VA educational benefits are federal benefits earned by a service member or veteran. Benefit entitlement can change due to length of service, discharge characterization, as well as home of record/place of entry.

Hazlewood Benefit (State of Texas)

The Hazlewood Act provides veterans with 150 credit hours to utilize in higher education.

Documents Needed for Each Hazlewood Submission

Benefit Type Description Documents Needed
Hazlewood-Standard Veteran utilizing own benefit (150 hours) DD214, Hazlewood Exemption Application, TX residency, Certificate of eligibility showing no federal educational benefits available, Meet SHSU’s Financial Aid GPA requirements
Hazlewood-Legacy Veteran shares 150 hours benefit with dependent DD214, Must be younger than 25, TX residency for both student and Veteran, Hazlewood Exemption Application, Proof of relationship, Selective Service for male dependents
Hazlewood - Non-Legacy (Disability) 150 hours for dependents of a veteran who is 100% disabled, MIA, or KIA DD214, 100% Permanent and Total VA disability rating, Hazlewood Exemption Application, Proof of relationship, Student must be Texas Resident

Note: Hazlewood does not pay for housing, meals, or book stipends. This waiver may not fully cover your tuition and fees.

Active Duty Students

If you wish to use Tuition Assistance, you must speak with your Educational Services Officer (ESO) or counselor at your base first.
